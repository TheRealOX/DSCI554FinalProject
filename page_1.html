Data Source

We adopt some intuitive, averaged features from Basketball Reference such as Field Goals Made, Three Points Made, Freethrows Made, Turnovers, and Rebounds. To find out whether these features played important roles in the NBA, we compared data of NBA Champions with their competitors from 2000 to 2018.

What We Found

Surprisingly, only one metric gives us some clues: among the top 10 teams who made the most averaged free throws, only two did not win the championship. On the other hand, most of these metrics did not explain why a team outperforms others: Cavaliers in 2017 made the third most averaged field goals in 18 years but still lost; Cavaliers in 2018 even made the most averaged rebounds whereas its opponent Warriors' averaged rebounds was ranked 25 across all data points. It was hard for us to accept that Field Goals Made is not an important factor to get the championship. So we pulled the Field Goals Percentage and Free-throw Percentage of the championships and compared them with the league average percentage.

One more step

After we compared the champions’ field goals and free-throw percentage with the rest of the league, we did not find any significant differences between the champions and the others: most of the time champions had a close or even lower Field Goals percentage than the rest of the league.
